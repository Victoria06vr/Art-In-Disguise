    Choosing The Right Spot

    Mihály Kolodko places his mini-statues in locations that add meaning, humor, or a small story to the sculpture. He looks for spots where the surroundings can enhance the character—whether through a symbolic link, a cultural reference, or a playful twist.

    Kolodko places his mini-statues in everyday public spaces like railings, bridges, and benches, turning discovery into part of the experience. The figures often interact with their surroundings, making them feel naturally connected to the city. Their locations reflect local history or Hungarian culture, encouraging people to notice overlooked details and view familiar places with fresh curiosity.
