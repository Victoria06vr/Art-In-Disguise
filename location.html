    Choosing the Right Spot

    Mihály Kolodko places his mini-statues in locations that add meaning, humor, or a small story to the sculpture. He looks for spots where the surroundings can enhance the character—whether through a symbolic link, a cultural reference, or a playful twist.

    He places his mini-statues in everyday public spots like railings, bridges, and benches—areas people often overlook. This makes discovery part of the experience. Many of the figures “interact” with their surroundings, such as sitting on a ledge or looking over water, so they feel naturally connected to the city. Kolodko’s locations often reflect local history or Hungarian culture, always with a playful touch. By choosing subtle and accessible places, he encourages people to notice small details and see familiar areas with renewed curiosity.
